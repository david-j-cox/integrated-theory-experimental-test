Informed Consent

Study Title: Integrated Theory of Behavior Networks: Decision-Making Under Dynamic Conditions

Principal Investigator: David J. Cox, Ph.D., Endicott College

You are being invited to participate in a research study. Please read the following information carefully before deciding whether to take part.

Purpose of the Research: This study investigates how people make repeated choices when the consequences of those choices change unpredictably over time. We are testing an integrated theoretical model that combines concepts from behavior analysis and decision-making research. Your responses will help us understand how people adapt to changing decision environments.

What You Will Do: You will play a computer-based foraging task lasting approximately 18 minutes total. In this task, you will see five buttons on the screen and can click any button at any time. Each button may earn you points, but the point value for each button will change throughout the game in ways that are not announced to you. Your goal is to earn as many points as possible. The task is divided into two blocks of approximately 7 minutes each, with a 30-second break in between. You will also experience brief pauses (quiescence periods) during which the game temporarily becomes unresponsive and you cannot earn points, in order to measure how you respond when rewards are unavailable.

Risks: There are no known risks to you beyond those of everyday computer use. You may experience mild boredom or fatigue during the task. No harmful stimuli are presented.

Benefits: There are no direct benefits to your participation. However, your data will contribute to scientific understanding of decision-making and behavior dynamics, which may inform future research on learning and adaptation in humans.

Confidentiality: Your responses will be recorded and associated with an anonymous identifier. Only your Prolific ID will be collected, and this is stored separately from your task responses. No other personally identifying information will be collected. All data is stored securely and will be used only for research purposes.

Voluntary Participation: Your participation is entirely voluntary. You may withdraw at any time by closing your browser, with no penalty or loss of compensation. Withdrawal will not affect your relationship with Prolific or Endicott College.

Contact Information: If you have questions about this study, you may contact the Principal Investigator, David J. Cox, at dcox@endicott.edu. If you have questions about your rights as a research participant, you may contact the Endicott College Institutional Review Board.
